When we think about traditional health care today, we mostly associate it with drugs and surgery. Without doubt, medicine has had considerable success in treating bacterial infections, viruses, and developed sound apparatus of emergency procedures. However, most chronic maladies remain de facto incurable. Drugs often surpress symptoms rather than eliminate the cause of desease producing numerous side effects along the way. Removing parts of one's body is not a long-term answer either especially if one aspires to live life to the fullest. Worst of all, the costs of traditional healthcare are mounting quickly, with fewer than ever people able to afford it.

Natural medicine has been in use for thousands of years. It is holistic as it always looks for the root cause of the problem. It does not usually have side effects, is extremely low-cost and non-invasive. Unfortunately, being used by millions of people worldwide, natural medicine remains a frontier area of medical research. In Mendel we believe that this situation has to change. We decided to build a dedicated online platform to bring together open-minded medical practitioners and naturopaths to bring new momentum into natural medicine clinical research. Naturopaths often practice empirically based on observation, common sense, and esoteric knowledge. By using this website they could form groups to fund specific clinical reseach related to their area of expertise. Results of such research could place natural medicine practice on solid scientific foundation and would not be biased by pharmaceutical companies. Many alternative medicine companies and practitioners could benefit from enhanced credibility. Medical researchers, on the other hand, could improve funding access for out-of-mainstream projects and acquire better understanding as to which factors would be critical to assess in the respective clinical trial.

FAQ

Which services does this website provide?
We have a crowdfunding platform for natural medicine research projects and a forum to bring together traditional clinical research teams and naturopahts.
What does a contributor get?
A contributor obtains up-to-date information on the progress of a study, copies of official documents from the study, and the final research report.
What is the interest of a medical researcher?
A medical researcher or a medical research team can benefit by covering all or part of the required funding through crowd donations. The research topic or trial could also be refined based on feedback of practising naturopaths.
